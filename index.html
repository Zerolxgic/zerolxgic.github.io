About Me

I'm Zerrius, a self-taught builder focused on practical AI systems, workflow design, and tools that help real work hold its shape.

Right now, most of that work centers on Addison, a personal state machine and local agent system built around deterministic artifact memory, stateless inference, and human-governed control.

WBTSR is the workflow I pulled out of building Addison. It keeps work bounded, separates building from auditing, and treats truth, closeout, and rehydration as part of finishing the job.

I have ADHD, so I build systems that survive context loss, stay honest about scope, and help me keep moving without pretending automation should run the whole show.

I'm still learning in public and still building this up as I go. This site is a simple place to share the work as it gets clearer, tighter, and more real.
